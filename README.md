# Wedding Reception Playlist

An AI-assisted wedding reception playlist curated for a multi-generational crowd. The project produced a 100-song master list organized by genre vibe and selection tier, along with song suggestions for three special parent dances.

---

## Playlist Overview

| Metric | Count |
|--------|-------|
| Total songs | 100 |
| Genre vibes | 8 |
| Selection tiers | 4 |
| Special dance suggestion categories | 3 |

### Genre Vibes

- R&B / Neo-Soul
- Hip-Hop
- Dance Pop
- Funk / Disco
- Classic Rock
- 90s R&B
- Country
- Romantic

### Selection Tiers

| Tier | Count | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Required | 10 | Songs specified by the couple — must appear in the final playlist |
| Standard | 70 | Core curated selections across all vibes |
| Blending | 8 | Songs that sit at genre intersections, used to smooth transitions between sections |
| Extreme | 12 | Songs that push each genre toward its harder, rawer, or more traditional edge |

## Special Dance Suggestions

Song recommendations are provided for three parent dances:

- Father of the Bride / Bride Dance
- Mother of the Bride / Bride Dance
- Mother of the Groom / Groom Dance

These are not included in the 100-song count and are intended as a separate shortlist for the couple to choose from.

## Curation Methodology

### Genre Diversity as the Foundation

The couple's required songs already spanned classic rock, hip-hop, R&B, funk, country, and neo-soul — signaling they wanted a diverse reception, not a single-genre party. Every additional song was chosen to reinforce one of those established lanes rather than introduce something foreign to the set.

### Generational Range

With guests spanning multiple generations, the playlist intentionally covers five decades of music. Classic soul and rock serve older guests; contemporary hip-hop, R&B, and dance pop serve the couple and their peers. Cross-generational crowd-pleasers — songs broadly recognized regardless of age — were included deliberately to serve as connective tissue between sections.

### Dance Floor Energy Arc

A reception needs pacing. The playlist is designed with a loose arc in mind:

1. **Opening / Dinner** — Romantic and Country selections, lower energy, background-friendly
2. **Warm-up** — 90s R&B and Neo-Soul, easing guests onto the floor
3. **Build** — Hip-Hop and Funk/Disco, raising the energy
4. **Peak** — Dance Pop and high-energy selections at full intensity
5. **Wind-down** — Romantic closers and slower selections to end the night

The 100-song depth gives a DJ flexibility to read the room and stretch or compress each phase without repeating.

### Blending Songs

Eight songs were selected specifically because they sit at genre intersections. These serve as pivot points a DJ can use to move between sections without a jarring jump — for example, moving from a country block into hip-hop, or from soul into rock, without losing the crowd's momentum.

### Genre-Extreme Songs

Twelve songs push each genre toward its harder, rawer, or more traditional edge. These are intentional additions for guests who want real depth in a given genre — heavier hip-hop, classic country without pop polish, full-throttle rock, and deep-cut funk/soul. They reward the right crowd at the right moment and should be deployed deliberately rather than scattered throughout.

### Bride's Dance Background

Because the bride has a competitive dance background, selections favor strong rhythmic foundations — syncopated bass lines and groove-heavy arrangements — not just lyrical content. The Funk/Disco and 90s R&B sections in particular were built to reward someone who knows how to move to a pocket groove.
